# L3: R&D Operations Manager

> I obsess over critical R&D activities, cycles, and leadership working groups. These typically involve how we communicate, coordinate plans, and amplify signals. I drive activities, ensuring that people understand what needs to be done, and that critical commitments are followed through. Through each cycle, I look for ways to improve these activities to make better use of our people’s efforts.

- **Planning horizon**: Two Quarters
- **Impact radius**: R&D Leaders
- **Evaluation**: Manager
- **Responsibility and direction needed**: Runs defined activities, and collects feedback to identify opportunities to improve.

## 🤹 Run the System: I ensure that R&D activities, cycles, and working groups are well run and critical commitments are followed through.

- I break down and document cycles into timelines, activities, contributors, and responsibilities.
- I document how we run activities so contributors understand their role, what needs to be done and by when.
- I lead kick-offs, check-ins, and wrap-ups for complex activities, ensuring we have an agenda and that pre-work has been completed.
- I follow up on critical actions to make sure they are completed.

<details>
<summary>Examples</summary>

- I ensured the R&D operational calendar is up to date.
- I updated the quarterly planning and RLT-GLT stocktake playbooks in response to things that went wrong.
- I sent reminders to contributes that outlined what they need to prepare for a milestone in the budget forecast update cycle
- I captured key decisions and actions at a working group in the agenda.
- I clarified a “we should” action into specific what, by whom, and by when.
- I tagged an action as critical, then followed up regularly with the owner until it was completed.

</details>

## 👷 Improve the System: I lead projects to make R&D operations more efficient and effective.

- I capture feedback, assess health, and identify opportunities that could improve existing processes.
- I look for ways to remove wasted effort, like extra steps or delays, through streamlined procedures and automation of routine tasks.
- I find ways to work together, share information, and break down barriers to solve problems faster.
- I pitch ideas and experiments for processes, drawing on inspiration from other organizations.

<details>
<summary>Examples</summary>

- I organized a retrospective after quarterly planning, summarized the key improvement opportunities, then took steps to improve the playbook.
- I automated a regular check on overdue tasks using automation tooling like Zapier, Claude, Slack bots.
- I experimented with AI tooling to post digests that recapped the last seven days of discussions within a working group.
- I standardized how a working group tracks actions using [insert tool], and creating a way for anyone to add items to the list as part of async conversations in Slack.

</details>
